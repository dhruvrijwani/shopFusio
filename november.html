Key Insights & Takeaways

Critical factors impacting November performance

Primary Challenges

  • The month began with a high volume of returns and cancellations, which significantly impacted the overall daily revenue.
  • Even excluding cancellations, performance remained low due to a sharp increase in CPC and CPM—up by more than 47% and 32% respectively.
  • We believe certain external factors, including the post-Diwali period, also contributed to the decline.
  • A significant drop in performance was also noticed after product prices were increased by 15–20%, especially post 19th November.
  • While most other metrics remained relatively stable or showed minimal variation, the rise in CPC and CPM had the most substantial negative impact.

Actions Taken to Improve Performance

  • To improve performance, we implemented several website-level optimizations such as introducing partial COD for high-risk orders to reduce fake cancellations and RTOs, removing the 10% welcome offer for new users, increasing the minimum cart value to ₹5,000 for the 30% discount to push higher AOV orders, and raising the prepaid discount to 10% to boost prepaid conversions.
  • However, these adjustments did not produce the expected improvements, so all changes were eventually reverted.
  • We also tested new audiences and ad variations to identify potential winners, including multiple interest-based audiences, 1% lookalike audiences of delivered orders, and narrow retargeting segments like website visitors from the last 30 days and Instagram engagers from the last 60 days.
  • New ads were created with a focus on strong-performing catalogue sets—specifically high AOV sets above ₹1,800 and the best-selling earrings catalogue set.
  • We also produced new creatives highlighting best-selling products and high-AOV necklaces, but performance continued to remain weak.

Key Insights

  • Returns and cancellations significantly impacted overall performance metrics and daily revenue generation.
  • CPC and CPM increased sharply by 47% and 32% respectively, directly affecting campaign efficiency.
  • Post-Diwali period and seasonal factors contributed to lower engagement and conversion rates.
  • Price increases of 15-20% implemented after November 19th led to noticeable performance decline.
  • Website optimization attempts including partial COD, revised discount structures, and cart value adjustments did not yield expected results.
  • New audience testing including interest-based segments, 1% LAL audiences, and retargeting campaigns showed inconsistent performance.
  • High AOV product sets (₹1,800+) and best-selling earrings catalogue did not perform as anticipated.

Returns & Cancellations

Impact analysis and root causes

Critical Issues

  • Many returns and RTOs recorded in November were from orders originally placed in early October or even earlier, which skewed November's numbers.
  • Another concerning factor was the rise in RTOs caused by delivery partner issues, including fake delivery attempts, poor follow-up with COD customers, and fake items being returned in RTO shipments.
